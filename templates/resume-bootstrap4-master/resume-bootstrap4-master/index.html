Technical Writing

Charlotte Freeman · WILSON 11-28 · Section 5, 1:10pm · charlotte.freeman@montana.edu

Course Description

Focuses on kinds of writing done in technical or business environments: business letters, proposals, formal reports, technical presentations, user manuals, etc. Prepares students for technical writing in a range of disciplines and with attention to the social implications of technology.

Course Specifics

Learning Outcomes

What you are going to learn
  • Learn and understand the core elements of proper tech writing.
  • Become comfortable with collaboration tools.
  • Learn the components of a functional team and how to be a valuable team mate.

Prerequisites

What is required for learning
  • Writ 101
  • Be competent and correct in writing English sentences. If written English is still a struggle for you, I recommend taking a writing-intensive course like Writing 211 before taking this course. Otherwise you should commit to working with the Writing Center or other campus resources to assure your writing skills are up to par.

Participation, Deliverables, and Homework

What you will be working on
  • Attendance and participation is mandatory and will be checked at the beginning of class every day.
  • Deliverables are our main projects and consist of 70% group work and 30% personal work.
  • Homework is due through the Discussion section on the class page of D2L.

Course Policies

  • All assignments are due by the time posted on D2L on the due date. Late papers will not earn full credit (Maximum credit drops one letter grade per calendar day an assignment is late: 1 day=B, 2 days=C, etc.). It is your responsibility to contact me about late work. Communication is key.
  • Out-of-class work will be submitted via D2L If you miss a D2L deadline, then it is too late to turn in the work. if an assignment is lost or missing, you must provide another copy no matter whose fault it is. Be sure to save your D2L submission receipts.
  • Your work in this class is always public. Don’t submit writing you can’t let us all read.
  • Personal electronic device (PED) policy: Because of the collaborative nature of the work we’re doing, I ask you to bring smart phones/tablets/laptops to class. However, I expect that you will use them for class purposes and activities. Misuse of PED will have a negative effect on your participation grade at the end of the semester.
  • Plagiarism: You are always responsible for acknowledging source material in your writing. Cheating—whether by claiming others’ work as your own (fraud) or fabricating material—will result in a course grade of F and a report to the Dean of Students.
  • I reserve final discretion in adjusting grades to account for unanticipated circumstances.
  • I may use copies of your work, anonymously, for samples in future classes or for research.
